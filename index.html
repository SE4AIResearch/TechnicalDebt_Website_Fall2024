Technical Debt

Fall 2024 → Spring 2025 Extension

About

Self-Admitted Technical Debt (SATD) is a transparent form of technical debt where the contributor explicitly documents the debt’s location. This admission usually includes a description of a known or potential defect or a statement outlining the remaining work. For each detected SATD, the tool reports all associated changes, including text updates and eventual removal. The goal of SATD-Bailiff is to assist researchers and practitioners in effectively tracking SATD instances and offering a reliable, easily extendable tool.

Improvements

The aim of our project is to further improve on the SATD-Bailif tool by creating a plugin version which will integrate seamlessly with IntelliJ, a popular IDE. This will allow developers to identify and track SATD directly within their coding environment. This enhancement aims to provide real-time feedback and streamline the process of managing technical debt, making it more accessible and efficient for developers.
